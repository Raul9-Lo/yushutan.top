History

A Forty-Three Year Legacy

1978

SR500 Debut

Yamaha launches the SR500 — 499cc, air-cooled, single-cylinder, kick-start only. Inspired by the XT500 enduro, it creates a new category: the modern classic.

1979

SR400 Introduced

Same spirit, smaller displacement for the Japanese market. This would become Yamaha's longest-running production model — 43 years of continuous manufacturing.

1988

Electronic Ignition

First major update brings electronic ignition. The SR's simple, modular design earns a cult following among custom builders.

2001

35th Anniversary

Special edition models released. The SR community thrives — dedicated magazines like The Sound of Singles SR document the culture.

2021

Final Edition

After 43 years, Yamaha announces the SR400 Final Edition. The last mass-produced kick-start motorcycle in history. A bittersweet end to an icon.
