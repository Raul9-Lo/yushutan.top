CHAPTER 02

SR400 Journal

ザ・サウンド・オブ・シングルス

A visual journey through The Sound of Singles SR — the definitive Japanese magazine dedicated to Yamaha's legendary single-cylinder motorcycle. From its 1978 debut to the final edition in 2021.

HISTORY

A 43-Year Legacy

1978

SR500 Debut

Yamaha launches the SR500 with a 499cc air-cooled single-cylinder engine. Kick-start only. Inspired by the XT500 enduro, it creates a new category: the modern classic.

1979

SR400 Introduction

The SR400 is born — same spirit, smaller displacement for the Japanese market. This would become the longest-running production motorcycle in Yamaha's history.

1988

First Major Update

Electronic ignition introduced. The SR begins to develop a cult following among custom builders who appreciate its simple, modular design.

2001

35th Anniversary

Special edition models released. The SR community in Japan is stronger than ever, with dedicated magazines like "The Sound of Singles SR" chronicling the culture.

2021

Final Edition

After 43 years of continuous production, Yamaha announces the SR400 Final Edition. A bittersweet farewell to the longest-running big single in history. The last kick-start motorcycle in mass production.
