CHAPTER 01

The Rider

騎手より

My name is Yushu. I live in Conghua, a district in northern Guangzhou known for its hot springs, lychee orchards, and winding mountain roads — the kind of roads that beg to be ridden.

The Yamaha SR400 is not just a motorcycle. It's a forty-year-old design that refuses to die because it never needed to change. Air-cooled, single-cylinder, kick-start only. In an era of ride-by-wire and traction control, the SR asks you to be present. Every cold start is a small ritual. Every ride is a conversation between rider and machine.

SR400は単なるバイクではない。空冷・単気筒・キック始動。変わる必要のなかった、変わらなかった40年の設計。

Coffee, to me, is the same. The best cup doesn't come from a super-automatic machine — it comes from understanding the bean, the grind, the water temperature, the pour. Whether it's a single-origin Ethiopian Yirgacheffe or a dark-roast Sumatran, the process matters as much as the result.

This site is a collection of inspirations — pages from Japanese magazines that celebrate SR400 culture and the art of coffee, interpreted through the lens of a rider in southern China.

広州の山道をSR400で走り、一杯のハンドドリップを淹れる。それが私の日常です。

"Live in the present — that's what the SR teaches you. You can't rush a kick-start."

キックを急いではいけない。今を生きろ。

Yushu Tan
