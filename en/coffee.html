CHAPTER 03

Coffee Journal

珈琲の世界

Exploring Japanese coffee culture through the pages of FOOD DICTIONARY コーヒー, Best of Coffee (Discover Japan), and All About Coffee. From bean origins to brewing mastery.

The Bean

Arabica vs Robusta. Single origin vs blends. Understanding the terroir — from Ethiopian highlands to Sumatran jungles. Every bean tells a story of altitude, soil, and processing method.

The Brew

Pour-over, French press, siphon, espresso. Each method extracts differently. The Japanese approach to hand-drip (ハンドドリップ) is a meditative practice — water temperature, flow rate, bloom time, all precisely controlled.

The Roast

Light, medium, dark. City roast, full city, French roast. Japanese roasters are known for deep-roast styles (深煎り) that bring out rich, chocolatey notes while preserving clarity.

The Roasters

Japan's artisan coffee scene — from century-old kissaten to third-wave pioneers. Profiles of master roasters who treat coffee with the same reverence as tea ceremony.
